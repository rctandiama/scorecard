Capital Equipment Request Evaluation

Section 1: Strategic Impact and Patient Benefit

This section assesses the investment's transformative potential for strategic positioning, patient care outcomes, regional presence, clinical advancement, and health equity.

1.1 Equipment Type Impact (0-5 points)
Strategic nature of the capital investment. Highest scores for transformative equipment establishing regional leadership.
1.2 Patient Outcome Transformation (0-5 points)
How the equipment fundamentally improves patient outcomes, reduces complications, enables minimally invasive procedures, or provides life-saving capabilities.
1.3 Regional Service Leadership (0-4 points)
How the equipment positions the organization as a regional referral center, attracts patients from wider geographic areas, or establishes unique service capabilities.
1.4 Clinical Excellence and Innovation (0-4 points)
How the equipment enables cutting-edge procedures, supports research, attracts top medical talent, or advances the organization's reputation for clinical excellence.
1.5 Equity and Access Considerations (0-4 points)
How the investment supports health equity, underserved populations, or geographic access within the Fraser Health region.

Section 2: Strategic Need and Market Position

This section assesses the strategic necessity of the investment, competitive positioning, and alignment with long-term organizational goals.

2.1 Strategic Urgency and Market Imperative (0-5 points)
Strategic urgency of this investment, including competitive threats, market opportunities, or regulatory requirements making it essential for organizational success.
2.2 Patient Volume and Revenue Potential (0-5 points)
Comprehensive data on projected patient volumes, revenue generation, market capture, and return on investment for this capital equipment.
2.3 Alignment with Strategic Plan (0-4 points)
How this investment directly supports the organization's strategic plan, mission, vision, and long-term goals.
2.4 Risk Assessment and Mitigation (0-3 points)
Identifies potential risks associated with this investment and describes comprehensive mitigation strategies.
2.5 Stakeholder Engagement (0-3 points)
Extent of consultation with physician/clinical stakeholders and patient involvement in the needs assessment.

Section 3: Implementation and Long-Term Viability

This section evaluates the organization's capacity to successfully implement and sustain this major capital investment over its operational lifetime.

3.1 Implementation Readiness and Infrastructure (0-5 points)
Comprehensive readiness including facility modifications, utilities, regulatory approvals, integration with existing systems, and project management capabilities.
3.2 Comprehensive Support and Maintenance (0-4 points)
Detailed plans for vendor support, service contracts, preventive maintenance, technical expertise, spare parts inventory, and lifecycle management.
3.3 Specialized Training and Competency Development (0-4 points)
Comprehensive training programs for clinical and technical staff, ongoing education, competency validation, and knowledge transfer strategies.
3.4 Long-Term Financial Sustainability (0-5 points)
Detailed financial projections including operating costs, revenue streams, cost savings, depreciation, replacement planning, and long-term ROI analysis.
3.5 Quality and Outcome Measurement (0-3 points)
Specific metrics and monitoring systems to measure the success of this investment, including clinical outcomes, quality indicators, and performance benchmarks.
3.6 Innovation Incentives and Scalability (0-3 points)
Potential for this equipment to enable piloting new approaches or its scalability for broader organizational impact.

Major Equipment Request Evaluation

Section 1: Impact and Patient Benefit

This section evaluates how the equipment will directly improve patient care, enhance clinical outcomes, and expand service capabilities within your department.

1.1 Type of Equipment Request (0-5 points)
Evaluate whether this is a replacement, upgrade, or new equipment acquisition. New equipment that enables entirely new capabilities receives the highest score.
1.2 Anticipated Patient Care and Experience Improvement (0-5 points)
Describe specific, measurable improvements to patient care, such as reduced procedure times, improved diagnostic accuracy, enhanced comfort, or better clinical outcomes.
1.3 Expansion/Enhancement of Services (0-4 points)
Explain how this equipment will expand your department's capacity or enable new services. Include projected increases in patient volume or new procedures that will become available.
1.4 Impact on Staff Efficiency and Workflow (0-3 points)
Describe how this equipment will improve staff productivity, reduce manual tasks, streamline workflows, or enhance the overall efficiency of clinical operations.

Section 2: Demonstrated Need and Justification

This section assesses the urgency and necessity of the equipment request, including current problems, patient demand, and alignment with organizational priorities.

2.1 Urgency of Need (0-5 points)
Explain the current problems or limitations that make this equipment necessary. Include specific examples of how the lack of this equipment is affecting patient care or operations.
2.2 Patient Volume and Demand (0-4 points)
Provide current usage statistics and projected patient volume data. Include trends showing increasing demand and how this equipment will help meet that demand.
2.3 Alignment with Hospital Priorities (0-3 points)
Explain how this equipment request supports the hospital's strategic plan, quality initiatives, or departmental goals. Reference specific organizational priorities.
2.4 Alternative Solutions Considered (0-2 points)
Describe other options that were considered, such as leasing, refurbishing existing equipment, or alternative vendors. Explain why this specific solution was chosen.

Section 3: Feasibility and Sustainability

This section evaluates your department's readiness to implement and sustain the equipment, including operational planning, training, and long-term financial considerations.

3.1 Operational Readiness (0-5 points)
Assess your department's readiness for implementation, including space requirements, infrastructure needs, regulatory compliance, and integration with existing systems.
3.2 Maintenance and Support Plan (0-4 points)
Outline your plan for ongoing maintenance, including service contracts, preventive maintenance schedules, and technical support arrangements. Include estimated annual costs.
3.3 Staff Training and Competency (0-3 points)
Describe your training plan for staff who will use this equipment, including initial training, ongoing education, and competency validation processes.
3.4 Financial Sustainability (0-5 points)
Provide a detailed financial plan showing how ongoing costs will be covered, including revenue projections, cost savings, and budget allocations for the equipment's lifecycle.
